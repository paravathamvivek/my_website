Virat Kohli

Indian Cricketer

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Kohli is widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world. He made his international debut in 2008 and quickly rose through the ranks with his aggressive batting style and consistent performances. Kohli has numerous records to his name, including being the fastest to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs). He has also been awarded the ICC ODI Player of the Year multiple times and has received the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year. In addition to his cricketing achievements, Kohli is known for his fitness regime and dedication to the sport. He has a massive fan following and is considered a role model for aspiring cricketers around the world.

Major international records and milestones

Most ODI Centuries:
With 51 centuries, Kohli holds the world record for the most hundreds in One Day International cricket.

Second-Highest ODI Run-Scorer:
He is currently second on the list of all-time leading run-scorers in ODIs, having surpassed Kumar Sangakkara in October 2025.

Highest White-Ball Run-Scorer:
In October 2025, Kohli surpassed Sachin Tendulkar to become the highest run-scorer in the history of white-ball cricket (ODIs and T20Is combined), with a total of 18,443 runs.

Player of the Tournament Awards:
He won the Player of the Tournament award at the 2023 ODI World Cup and the 2024 T20 World Cup, as well as the 2014 and 2016 T20 World Cups.

IPL stats and history

Team:
Kohli has played his entire IPL career for the Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB).

Most Runs in IPL History:
He is the all-time highest run-scorer in the IPL, with 8,661 runs through the end of the 2025 season.

IPL Champion:
He was part of the RCB team that won the franchise's first-ever IPL title in 2025.

Orange Cap Winner:
Kohli has won the Orange Cap twice, in 2016 and 2024.

Most Runs in a Season:
His 973 runs in the 2016 season remains the record for the most runs scored by a player in a single IPL edition.

Significant events and legacy:

Under-19 World Cup:
Kohli first gained prominence by captaining India to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup.

Final International Achievements:

2024 T20 World Cup:
He scored a match-winning 76 in the final against South Africa, after which he announced his retirement from T20Is.

2025 ICC Champions Trophy:
Kohli was a key player in India's title win, scoring a century against Pakistan and becoming the fastest to 14,000 ODI runs.

2025 Test Retirement:
Citing a desire to focus on white-ball cricket and his family, Kohli retired from Test cricket in May 2025, after a 14-year career.
